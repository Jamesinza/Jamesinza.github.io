Non-Stationarity

Most real world data is not stable. Most models assume it is.

The Assumption Problem

Many learning algorithms implicitly assume that the data-generating process is stationary.

In practice, environments drift, regimes shift, and correlations decay.

Observed Effects

  • performance decay without clear warning signals
  • learned features becoming actively misleading
  • over fitting to transient patterns

Key Insight

Non stationarity is not noise. It is structure that moves.

Treating it as randomness guarantees delayed failure.

Research Direction

This motivates adaptive readouts, gating mechanisms, and models that track uncertainty rather than suppress it.
