Failure Modes

Patterns of breakdown observed across experiments.

Why Document Failure

Benchmarks hide failure. Real systems reveal it.

This page captures recurring breakdown patterns that appear when models are pushed outside idealized assumptions.

Common Failure Patterns

  • silent collapse of internal representations
  • overconfident predictions under distribution shift
  • emergent shortcuts that bypass intended structure
  • training dynamics that look stable until they are not

Meta-Failure

Some of the most dangerous failures do not reduce loss or accuracy. They reduce meaningful learning while preserving surface metrics.

Design Implication

Robust systems are not those that never fail, but those whose failure modes are predictable and recoverable.
